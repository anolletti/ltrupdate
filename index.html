Current Focus

We are currently focused on the K–4 continuum to ensure clarity, stability, and realistic expectations in early FSL programming.

Key Themes from Preliminary Consultations

  • Concrete & measurable outcomes in the K–4 continuum
  • Stability and consistency across classrooms
  • Continued and expanded use of French Language Opportunities in Rural Areas (FLORA)
  • Realistic expectations aligned with provincial assessment data
  • Reduced planning via rich, exemplary-practice resources

What is FLORA?

French Language Opportunities in Rural Areas (FLORA) was developed to provide equitable access to French Second Language (FSL) opportunities in rural settings.

  • Recognizes educators’ varied French proficiency and pedagogical training
  • Serves as an optional, flexible resource adaptable to diverse classrooms

Advantages of FLORA as a Prescribed Resource

  • Vetted by FLS experts
  • Already implemented within the system
  • Embeds exemplary FSL teaching practices
  • Provides differentiated and engaging materials (songs, puppets, conversations, progressive language)
  • Written in Canadian French for NB learners
  • Accessible and easy to integrate

Current Work in Progress

  • Timeline Development: Establishing key milestones for K–10 rollout
  • Outcome Enhancement: Strengthening K–4 outcomes for clarity, accountability, and assessment
  • Cross-Curricular Integration: Identifying connections to maximize French exposure across subjects
  • Rebranding: French Language Opportunities in Rural Areas → French Language Opportunities Reaching All
  • Sustainability: Reducing dependence on technology to improve long-term accessibility
  • Resource Inventory: Reviewing current materials and gaps
  • Professional Learning: Needs assessment and online module development for educators
  • Leadership Guidelines: Tools for school leaders to support classroom teachers (e.g., FSL teacher recommended)
